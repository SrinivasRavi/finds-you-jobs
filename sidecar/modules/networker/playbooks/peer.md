# Audience playbook — Peer (fellow IC on the team/function)

<!-- US-NW-09 / FR-REF-01 audience: Peer. Bound to a contact tagged `peer`
(engineer, developer, scientist, designer — an individual contributor in the
role's function). -->

**Who they are.** A current individual contributor in the same function as the
target role — likely a future teammate. They are the most approachable referrer
and the most credible judge of whether you'd fit the team.

**The angle.** Peer-to-peer, low-pressure, craft-first. Lead with the concrete
overlap between your work and theirs (same stack, same problem space). Ask for a
quick take on the team and, if it feels right, a referral — framed as "would you
be open to referring me / pointing me to the right person", never a demand.

**Emphasize:** the 1–2 master proof points closest to the team's actual work
(shared technologies, comparable systems you've built). Specificity earns a reply.

**Tone:** collegial, concise, unpretentious. You're talking to someone who does
what you do.

**Cold (connection note):** one line of shared ground + the role + a light ask.
**Warm (DM):** you can reference that you're already connected and go a touch
deeper on the overlap before the ask.

**Avoid:** flattery, credential-dumping, or implying you already have an in.
