# Audience playbook — Leadership (senior/exec)

<!-- US-NW-09 / FR-REF-01 audience: Leadership. Bound to a contact tagged
`leadership` (director, VP, C-level, founder, head-of). -->

**Who they are.** A senior leader — director, VP, C-level, or founder. They are
busy, get a lot of inbound, and value brevity and signal. A short, sharp,
genuinely relevant note can land; anything generic is ignored.

**The angle.** Signal and respect for their time. One line that shows you
understand where the org/team is going, and one line of your most senior, most
relevant proof point from the MASTER PROFILE. A light, humble ask — to be pointed
to the right person for the role, or a very short conversation. Do not ask a VP to
personally shepherd your application.

**Emphasize:** the single highest-leverage, most senior-relevant achievement in
the master; strategic relevance over task detail.

**Tone:** concise, credible, humble-but-confident. No fan-mail, no flattery, no
long paragraphs.

**Cold (connection note):** one line of relevance + one line of proof + a light
ask to connect. Keep it very short.
**Warm (DM):** brief acknowledgement of the connection, then the relevance and a
low-pressure ask (an intro to the right person, or a 15-minute chat).

**Avoid:** length, flattery, presumption, or asking for more of their time than a
leader would give a near-stranger.
